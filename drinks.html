PLACES TO DRINK IN LEEDS

Top 7 bars for drinks

1. Bundobust

Bringing together scrumptious Indian street food with lashings of craft beer, Bundobust is a surefire winner when you’re going out in Leeds. Part bar, part restaurant and full-on delicious, it’s your pre-party dinner and drinks all in one. The beer list is ever-changing but always celebrates local breweries with Leeds legends Northern Monk, Kirkstall Brewery and North Brewing Co. regularly gracing the taps.

Bundobust, 6 Mill Hill, Leeds, West Yorkshire, LS1 5DQ. Open from 8am on weekdays and from 9am on weekends.

2. Belgrave Music Hall

Three storeys of boozy revelry await at Belgrave Music Hall. They’ve have taken the term ‘bar’ and stretched it to the extreme. Sure, there are cask ales to glug across the venue’s two bars. Sure, you can chill on a picnic bench to neck a frozen cocktail or two. Live music, retro games nights, free yoga, burlesque shows – Belgrave Music Hall is a bar firing on all cylinders.

Belgrave Music Hall, 1-1A Cross Belgrave Street, Leeds, West Yorkshire, LS2 8JP.Open from 9am Monday to Saturday.

3. The Domino Club

How many watering holes do you know hidden below a barbers? Not many, we bet, but The Domino Club is full of surprises. And it’s not just the clandestine location that makes it one of the best bars in Leeds. Once you’ve descended the back staircase inside Lord’s Barbering, you’ll find yourself faced with an oh-so-sultry underground jazz bar. Leather booths, flickering candles and a teensy stage where live music takes the reigns ’til the early hours every night of the week.

The Domino Club, 7 Grand Arcade, Leeds, West Yorkshire, LS1 6PG.

4. Oporto

On the hunt for a rip-roaring night out in Leeds where you can drink, dance and be merry until the sweat drips off the walls? Oporto is a must-visit for music lovers, a dive of a drinking den where live bands, good times and Jägermeisters reign supreme. A variety of musicians take to the stage here, from up-and-coming indie outfits to seasoned rock ’n’ rollers, ensuring there’s always a lively crowd to throw yourself into.

Oporto, 33 Call Lane, Leeds, West Yorkshire, LS1 7BT. Open from 8am on weekdays and 9am on weekends.

5. Mojo

Tip-top booze and great beats await at Mojo, a long-running favourite for thirsty punters in need of a rocking night out in Leeds. Mojo was the city’s original rock ’n’ roll dive bar and the good times have just kept on rolling. Neon signs, sticky floors and a foot-stomping selection of classics from The Rolling Stones to Stevie Wonder never fail to put you straight into the party spirit – the sort of place where you go for one, then end up staying all night long.

Mojo, 18 Merrion Street, Leeds, West Yorkshire, LS1 6PQ. Open from 7.30am Monday to Saturday and 9am on Sunday.

6. Water Lane Boathouse

Teetering on the edge of Granary Wharf’s waterfront, Water Lane Boathouse is only a few minutes’ stroll from Leeds Station, yet it feels a world away from the city centre hustle. Vast light-filled windows that look out over the water, a huge vaulted ceiling and hanging plants aplenty – this is the sort of bar that’s good for the soul. A former transhipment warehouse, there’s even an enormous beer garden close enough for you to dip your toes into the River Aire. It’s dog friendly too!

Water Lane Boathouse, Canal Wharf, Leeds, West Yorkshire, LS11 5PS. Open from 8.30am Tuesday to Saturday.

7. Outlaws Yacht club

Sometimes you don’t need fancy schmancy drinks, suited bar staff or an interior dripping in Victorian grandeur. Sometimes, you just want a night out in Leeds where you can plonk yourself down on a comfy sofa and while away the hours listening to vinyl records with a glass of your favourite poison in hand. Enter Outlaws Yacht Club, the cool-as-heck venue that’s part-bar, part-record shop and part-cafe all in one.

Outlaws Yacht Club, 38 New York Street, Leeds, West Yorkshire, LS2 7DY. Open from 9am every day.

8. Below Stairs

A subterranean sanctuary on South Parade, Below Stairs may initially be easy to miss, but it’s darned hard to stay away from once you know it’s there. Look out for the mysterious hanging sign, descend the staircase and you’ll enter a bar where everything, from the drinks to the decor, is 100% class. The deep blue walls, cosy rugs, deep armchairs and unique art on the walls make it the sorta spot you’ll want to come back to time and time again.

Below Stairs, 12 South Parade, Leeds, West Yorkshire, LS1 5AL. Open from 9am every day.

9. The Maven

Perhaps the worst best-kept secret in Leeds, The Maven continues to top best bar lists both in the city and further afield. Its dedication to first-rate cocktails in every colour of the rainbow means the speakeasy-style surrounds are far more than a mere gimmick. Discover the unmarked door on Call Lane, ascend the staircase and prepare to enter a dimly-lit world where 1920s drinking, dancing and downright decadence take centre stage.

The Maven, 1-3 Call Lane, Leeds, West Yorkshire, LS1 7DH. Open from 9am every day.
